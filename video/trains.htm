Trains don't run forever

Annunziato, Gemma de Julio, Pierucci, '95. Video, 8'. Slide-projection.

Three old men of Bassano in Teverina, a small village near Rome, describe an historical event: in 1943 the Germans bombed a train carrying explosives and the small village was destroyed. This image of a past war is interpreted by a modern observer facing the lack of historical memory of present time, in which new forms of violence arise. Digital images alternate with paintings and sounds of the past and of the present.

Kaos

Mauro Annunziato, '94-'96. Collection of digital images.

This collection includes a selected series of digital composition inspired to the creative power of chaos. These images derive from the experimentation of some chaotic mechanisms like attractors, fractals, iterated evolutions, cellular automata.

The term "chaos" have been used in the past of western cultures as the degeneration of the order. In spite of this negative feature, many artist and poets (like the ancient eastern cultures) have been fascinated by the chaos as the evocation of a condition full of the creative potential of the morphogenesis.

The paradigm of Deterministic Chaos (started on '70) has outlined as many complex phenomena without an apparent order have structured behavior under other observation space (in the attractor space). In this sense chaos is something we don't know instead of something without order.

Another interesting conclusion is that the chaotic systems are not predictable in the future and bifurcation (quick change of behavior) can occur for really little differences on the system conditions (initial conditions). For these reason the caotic systems continuously create new situations and new patterns. This is the base of my exploration about the creative chaos and it is also the base of the most of natural process and life evolution.

The series of images is subdivided in several sections (links at the top of the page) related to the concepts utilized for the generation of the basic structure of the compositions. For each series some example images are presented here.
